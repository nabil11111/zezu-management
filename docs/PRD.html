— 01 · Why this exists

Three shops.
One honest picture.

Right now the answers live in too many places. Who turned up today sits in a group chat. Stock lives in someone's head and a notebook by the fryer. Sales are three platform dashboards and a till roll. Salaries are an end-of-month headache built on memory.

None of that is broken because anyone is careless — it's what happens when a brand grows faster than its paperwork. ZEZU went from an idea to three sites and 20k+ happy customers; the tools behind the counter never got the same upgrade as the food.

We're proposing one private dashboard — on the office screen, the manager's phone, your phone — that always knows: who's in, what's selling, what's running low, and what everyone is owed.

It's not software for software's sake. Every screen in this proposal earns its place by replacing a group chat, a notebook, a spreadsheet, or a guess. If it doesn't save a manager time on a Friday night, it doesn't go in.

The pages that follow walk through the six things it will do, what a normal day feels like with it running, and exactly how we'd build and roll it out across Lodge Lane, Anfield and Wallasey — starting small, proving it in one shop, then switching on the rest.

— 02 · What it does

The six modules

01 / PEOPLE

Every person, sorted from day one

A profile for every member of staff across the three sites: role, site, contact, documents and pay rate. New hires get a ready-made onboarding checklist — documents in, training videos watched, trial shift done — so nobody starts a Friday shift half-set-up. Salaries aren't typed in from memory: they're calculated from the verified hours each person actually worked (more on that on the clock-in page).

The win Month-end pay takes minutes, not an evening — and every number can be traced back to a real shift.
02 / STOCK

Know tonight what you'll run out of tomorrow

At close, each site logs the day's usage in under a minute — tap the item, tap how much. The dashboard keeps the running level of everything from chicken to chilli jam, flags what's getting low, and turns the flags into a ready-to-send order list for each supplier. No more discovering at 6pm that the Salt & Pepper Chips are off the menu.

The win Ordering stops being a memory game — and you can finally see which site uses what, and at what pace.
03 / MENU

Every dish, on film

Each menu item gets its own short video — the plate, the portion, the finish — living inside the dashboard next to its recipe notes. New cooks train from it, every site plates the Dragon Chicken the same way, and you quietly build a library of mouth-watering footage the brand can reuse for socials and the website. (Filming is what we do — we'll shoot these with you.)

The win Consistency across three kitchens, plus a content library most takeaways would kill for.

Sales, the floor,
and the clock

04 / DAILY SALES

Uber · takeaway · dine-in, side by side

Each site enters its day in three numbers — Uber, takeaway over the counter, dine-in. The dashboard does the rest: today vs last week, site vs site, the channel mix, the best and worst days on record. You'll see in one glance whether a quiet Tuesday at Anfield is the weather or a trend — and which channel is actually growing.

The win Decisions about hours, staffing and promos get made on numbers, not vibes.
05 / LIVE VIEW

A miniature of the shop, in your pocket

The dashboard's home screen is a little living picture of ZEZU right now: each of the three sites as a card — who's clocked in on shift at this minute, today's takings so far, any stock flags raised. Wherever you are, ten seconds tells you the whole business is running as it should. When something's off — a shift with no one clocked in, a site that hasn't logged its day — it's visibly off.

The win You stop ringing shops to ask "everything alright?" — the answer is already on your phone.
06 / CLOCK-IN

Scan in, verified by the manager, paid to the minute

A ZEZU-branded QR poster by the door of each site. Staff scan it on their own phone to clock in and out; the shift manager gets a one-tap verification, so nobody can scan in from their sofa. Those verified hours build each person's timesheet automatically — and that timesheet is exactly what the People module uses to calculate salaries.

The win Hours are fair and provable in both directions — staff trust the count, and so do you.

— 03 · The UX, on a real day

How clocking in feels

1Scan

Aisha arrives at Lodge Lane, scans the poster by the door with her phone. No app to install — it just opens.

2Confirm

Her name and the time appear. One tap — "Start shift." It takes less time than tying an apron.

3Verify

The shift manager's screen shows "Aisha — clock-in, 16:58". One tap to confirm she's actually here.

4Paid right

At month end her verified hours × her rate are already added up. Nothing typed, nothing argued.

…and how a day runs

10:45
Open-up, Wallasey.

The opener scans in; the manager verifies. Your live view flips Wallasey to "open — 2 on shift".

12:30
Delivery arrives.

New stock is logged in as it comes off the van — thirty seconds, and the running levels update.

17:00
Friday rush builds.

Evening crew scan in one by one. You watch all three sites staff up from your sofa — no calls made.

22:40
Close-down.

Each manager logs the day's usage and the three sales numbers. Two minutes each, then lights off.

23:00
Your phone.

The whole day in one card per site: takings by channel, hours worked, and tomorrow's order list already drafted.

— 04 · The look & the trust

It will feel like Zezu,
not like software

We're building this on your design language — the red, the gold, the bold condensed type, the attitude. When a new hire opens their onboarding, or a manager checks the live view, it should feel like the same brand that made the shop worth queuing at.

Designed for real hands

  • Phone-first for staff — scan, tap, done, with wet-hands-sized buttons.
  • Big-screen mode for the office — the live view runs happily on a wall display.
  • Every daily task designed to take under two minutes, timed with your team.
  • English-plain labels; no jargon, no manuals needed.

Private & accountable

  • Every person gets their own sign-in — staff, managers and owners see different things.
  • Staff see their shifts and hours; managers run their site; owners see everything.
  • Every change is logged with a name and a time — quiet accountability, no drama.
  • Someone leaves? One tap and their access is gone that second.
